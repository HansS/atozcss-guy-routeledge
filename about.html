I’ve been teaching online and in person for the last 18 months and there’s something incredibly satisfying about sharing knowledge and empowering others.

As a contract front-end developer, I have the opportunity to work with a wide variety of clients on a range of different projects. But I’ve been working closely with General Assembly teaching front-end development in London and have been loving every minute of it. It’s been a whole new set of challenges but incredibly rewarding — and has proved to me just how much I love what I do.

Classroom teaching is great, but in an effort to spread the front-end love far and wide, I’ve put together this site and these videos on my favourite topic: CSS. The first series of 26 videos tackles a different CSS topic — one for each letter of the alphabet.

CSS is what got me hooked on web development, which in turn has given me an incredible quality of life; the freedom to work on the projects I choose, a healthy bank balance and numerous business opportunities. I learnt almost everything I know from watching other peoples’ blog posts and screencasts, so this is my chance to (hopefully) do the same for others.
